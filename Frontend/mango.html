Mango Tree Uses

Mango crop cultivation has many uses, including environmental benefits, health benefits, and economic uses.

Environmental Benefits

  • Soil erosion prevention: Mango trees help prevent soil erosion.
  • Soil fertility improvement: Mango trees improve soil fertility.
  • Carbon sequestration: Mango trees contribute to carbon sequestration.
  • Biodiversity support: Mango trees provide habitats for birds, insects, and other wildlife.

Health Benefits

  • Gut health: Mangoes are good sources of both soluble and insoluble fiber.
  • Weight management: Mangoes can support healthy weight goals.
  • Skin and hair health: Mangoes can boost hair and skin health.
  • Cholesterol: Mangoes can help lower cholesterol.
  • Blood pressure: Mangoes can help control blood pressure.
  • Cancer prevention: Mangoes may help prevent certain cancers, such as prostate cancer.
  • Anemia: Mangoes are beneficial for pregnant women and individuals with anemia due to their high iron content.
  • Anti-aging: Mangoes contain vitamins A and C, which promote collagen production.

Economic Uses

  • Fruit processing: Mangoes are used in fruit processing industries to make fruit-based drinks and beverages.
  • Timber: Mango wood is used for furniture making.

Common Diseases

Mango crops can be affected by many diseases, including anthracnose, powdery mildew, die back, and red rust.

Anthracnose

A fungal disease that can affect mango trees in the field and after harvest. Symptoms include blackish-brown specks on flowers and peduncles, and small black spots on panicles.

Powdery Mildew

A fungal disease that affects mango trees, panicles, fruits, and leaves. It is caused by the plant pathogen Oidium mangiferae. Mango is the only known host of this disease.

Other Diseases

  • Die back: Can be controlled by pruning diseased twigs and spraying with copper oxychloride.
  • Red rust: Causes defoliation of leaves and reduces photosynthetic activity.
  • Sooty mold: Common in orchards where mealy bugs, scale insects, and hoppers are not controlled.
  • Mango seed weevil: A pest that lays eggs in mango fruit, causing damage.
  • Nutritional deficiencies: Potassium deficiency can cause leaf margins to scorch, starting from the tip downwards.
  • Leaf spot: Causes dark brown spots in the center of leaves and lighter edges.

Pesticides/Fertilizers

  • Paclobutrazol: A plant growth regulator that reduces vegetative growth and promotes flowering.
  • Ethephon and NAA: Chemicals that help induce flowering, prevent flower bud shedding, and promote fruit ripening.
  • Movento: A pesticide that controls mango scale pests.
  • Copper fungicides: Used to treat mango scab and anthracnose.
  • Neem oil: Protects mangoes from fruit flies.
  • Lambda-Cyhalothrin: An insecticide used to control fruit flies.
  • Malathion: An insecticide used to control fruit flies.
  • Cypermethrin: An insecticide used to control fruit flies.
  • Other management practices: Use an 8-3-9 fertilizer rich in potassium, monitor orchards for fruit fly infestation, and install traps.
