Orange Plant Uses

Oranges are widely consumed for their nutritional benefits and have multiple industrial, medicinal, and agricultural applications.

Uses of Oranges

  • Juice: Oranges are commonly used to make fresh juice, rich in Vitamin C.
  • Marmalade: Seville oranges are often used to make marmalade.
  • Fresh Consumption: Oranges can be eaten raw and are an excellent source of antioxidants.

Uses of Orange By-Products

  • Peel Oil: Extracted from the peel for perfumes and aromatherapy.
  • Pectin: Used in food production for jams and jellies.
  • Animal Feed: Orange processing waste is used as livestock feed.
  • Honey Production: Orange blossoms contribute to high-quality honey.

Health Benefits

Oranges are rich in Vitamin A and C, boosting immunity, improving skin health, and offering strong antioxidant properties.

Common Diseases

  • Root Rot: Fungal infection leading to plant decay.
  • Gummosis: Causes chlorosis and die-back in citrus trees.
  • Citrus Dieback: Leads to fruit shortages and market price increases.
  • Anthracnose: Affects all growth stages due to fungal pathogens.

Pesticides and Fertilizers

Pesticides commonly used for orange cultivation include insecticides and fungicides.

Insecticides

  • Chlorpyriphos, Quinalphos, Profenophos, Cypermethrin
  • Lambda-cyhalothrin, Fenpropathrin, Bifenthrin

Fungicides

  • Imazalil, Azoxystrobin, Dimethomorph
  • Boscalid, Pyrimethanil, Thiabendazole
  • Fludioxonil, Pyraclostrobin

Pesticide Application

Farmers typically spray pesticides every 15-30 days, with up to 5-10 applications per season.

Pesticide Removal

Surface pesticide residues can be reduced using a baking soda solution, which helps break down harmful compounds.
