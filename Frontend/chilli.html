Chilli Plant Uses

Chillies have many uses, including cooking, medicine, and health benefits.

Cooking

  • Chillies add flavor, color, warmth, and spice to food.
  • Whole red chillies can be used to make spice blends for curries and stews.
  • Chillies can be used for tempering, which is adding spices to hot oil with herbs and aromatics.

Medicine

  • Capsaicin, the compound in chillies, is used in ointments.
  • Crushed chili leaves can be used to treat arthritis, shingles, and wounds.

Health Benefits

  • Chillies are rich in antioxidants, minerals, and vitamins.
  • Chillies may help with digestion, heart health, and metabolism.
  • Chillies may help with weight management, blood pressure, and immune function.
  • Chillies may help with headaches, migraines, and sinusitis.
  • Chillies may help with gut health and inflammation.
  • Chillies may help with cancer prevention.
  • However, chillies can cause stomach pain, diarrhea, and a burning sensation in some people.

Common Diseases

Chilli crops can be affected by many diseases, including viral, fungal, and bacterial diseases.

Fungal Diseases

  • Anthracnose: A fruit rot disease caused by the fungus Colletotrichum spp.
  • Phytophthora root rot: A soil-borne disease caused by the fungus Phytophthora capsici.
  • Black mold: Caused by several fungi, including Alternaria spp.
  • Fusarium wilt: A serious disease that can affect chilli crops.

Viral Diseases

  • Pepper leaf curl virus (PLCV): Affects the leaves and stem of the plant.
  • Pepper veinal mottle virus (PVMV): Affects the leaves and fruits of the plant.

Bacterial Diseases

  • Bacterial wilt: A disease that causes the leaves to droop and the entire plant to wilt.
  • Bacterial leaf spot: A disease that can affect chilli crops.

Other Diseases

  • Blossom-end rot: A disease that can be caused by inconsistent watering or calcium deficiency.
  • Sunburn: A disease that can be caused by intense sunlight.
  • Powdery mildew: A disease that can affect chilli crops.

Pesticides and Fertilizers

Pesticides:

  • Chlorantraniliprole: A synthetic insecticide based on an extract of the Ryania speciosa plant.
  • Emamectin Benzoate 5% SG: A non-systemic insecticide that penetrates leaf tissues.
  • Syngenta Verama Insecticide: An insecticide that controls insects and pests in crops.
  • Buprofezin 25% SC: An insecticide and insect growth regulator that controls sucking pests in chilies.
  • Cypermethrin: A broad-spectrum insecticide that kills targeted insects and beneficial insects.
  • Diafenthiuron 50% WP: A broad-spectrum insecticide that paralyzes pests until they die.
  • Carbosulfan 25% EC: A broad-spectrum insecticide that controls chewing and sucking pests.
  • FMC Rogor Insecticide: A broad-spectrum insecticide that controls a wide range of insect pests.
  • Capsaicin: An animal repellent that also repels insects and mites.
