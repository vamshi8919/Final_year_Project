Coconut Plant Uses

The coconut tree has many uses, including food, drink, oil, and building materials.

Food and Drink

  • Coconut water: A refreshing drink from the inside of the coconut.
  • Coconut milk: Made by mixing coconut water with the coconut flesh.
  • Coconut oil: An edible oil that comes from the coconut's meat, milk, and kernels.
  • Coconut meat: Used in many cuisines, especially in the south of India.

Building Materials

  • Coconut trunk: Used to make houses, furniture, bridges, and other infrastructure projects.
  • Coconut branches: Used to make brooms, roofing materials, toothpicks, and other tools.

Crafts

  • Coconut leaves: Used to make baskets, hats, mats, and other accessories.
  • Coconut fibers: Known as coir, these fibers are woven into mats, baskets, and used as stuffing for upholstery.
  • Coconut husks: Used to make ropes and coco peat, a growing medium for plants.

Other Uses

  • Coconut roots: Used as an oral mouthwash or toothbrush.
  • Coconut sap: Used to make an alcoholic drink called Toddy or palm wine.
  • Coconut essential oil: Used for massages and in cosmetics and skin care products.

Common Diseases

Coconut trees can get many diseases, including bud rot, leaf rot, stem bleeding, and root disease.

Bud Rot

The most common disease of coconut trees caused by Phytophthora palmivora, a chromistan. Can cause black spots on leaves, which fall off. If left untreated, can lead to no or limited yield.

Leaf Rot

Disfigures leaves and reduces yield and appears as water-soaked lesions on leaves.

Stem Bleeding

The stem may bleed a reddish-brown liquid that decays the tree's tissues.

Root Disease

Leaves may turn yellow and bend abnormally.

Other Coconut Diseases

  • Lethal Yellowing: The most destructive disease of coconut palms.
  • Ganoderma Wilt: A devastating disease that affects the production and productivity of palms.
  • Coconut Mites: An economically devastating mite that can destroy up to 60% of coconut production.
  • Coconut Root (Wilt): A non-lethal disease that affects the roots of coconut palms.
  • Kalimantan Wilt: A disease associated with the presence of a phytoplasma.

Fungi cause most of these diseases.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

IPM is a combination of cultural, mechanical, biological, and chemical practices that can be used to manage pests in coconut farms. IPM can also help farmers improve their knowledge of the ecosystem.

Organic Methods

Castor cake can also be used as a natural pest repellent and manure.
