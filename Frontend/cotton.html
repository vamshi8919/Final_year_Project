Cotton Plant Uses

Cotton is used for many products, including clothing, homewares, and industrial products. It's also used to make oil, rubber, and plastics.

Clothing

  • Shirts, jackets, and sportswear: Cotton is used to make all types of clothing, including t-shirts, jackets, sportswear, yoga wear, and activewear.
  • Bedsheets and curtains: Cotton is used for bedsheets and curtains.

Homewares

  • Coffee filters: Cotton is used to make coffee filters.
  • Bandages: Cotton is used to make bandages.
  • Book binding: Cotton is used for book binding.
  • Paper: Cotton is used to make paper.

Industrial Products

  • Tarpaulins and tents: Cotton is used to make tarpaulins and tents.
  • Hotel sheets: Cotton is used to make hotel sheets.
  • Army uniforms: Cotton is used to make army uniforms.
  • Astronauts' clothing: Cotton is used to make astronauts' clothing.

Oil, Rubber, and Plastics

  • Cottonseed oil: Cottonseed oil is used in food, cosmetics, soap, and margarine.
  • Rubber and plastics: Cotton seeds are crushed to make rubber and plastics.

Common Diseases

Cotton crops are susceptible to many diseases, including leaf spots, blights, and wilts.

Leaf Spots and Blights

  • Ascochyta blight: Also known as wet weather blight, this disease can cause considerable damage.
  • Bacterial blight: Also known as blackarm and angular leaf spot, this disease can cause considerable damage.
  • Cercospora leaf spot: This disease can cause premature defoliation, reduced yield, and lower fiber quality.
  • Alternaria leaf spot: This disease can cause considerable damage.

Wilts

  • Fusarium spp: This disease can cause wilted plants, stunted growth, and damaged vascular tissue.
  • Bacterial wilt: This disease can cause stunting, wilting, and withering, usually starting with younger leaves.

Pesticides and Fertilizers

Pesticides used in cotton crops include insecticides, herbicides, and defoliants. Some of the pesticides used in cotton crops include:

Insecticides

  • Asana
  • Assail
  • Agri-Mek 0.15 EC
  • Bacillus thuringiensis ssp. Aizawai
  • Chlorpyrifos
  • Fipronil
  • Imidacloprid
  • Malathion
  • Methyl demeton
  • Profenofos
  • Thiacloprid
  • Thiamethoxam

Herbicides

  • Glyphosate
  • Diuron
  • Tribufos

Defoliants

Used to prepare the crop for harvest.

Biopesticides

  • Eco-Bb®
  • Bb endophyte
  • Bolldex®
  • Delfin®

Some pesticides can be harmful to human health and the environment. For example, glyphosate, diuron, and tribufos are considered human carcinogens. Pesticides can also be found in the air, water, and food.
