Corn Plant Uses

Corn has many uses, including food, cooking oil, and thickener.

Food

  • Creamed corn: A dish made from sweetcorn and a milky liquid.
  • Baby corn: A young corn that's harvested before fertilization.
  • Dent corn: A type of field corn with a high starch content.
  • Bakery products: Corn is used in biscuits, bread, crackers, and more.

Beverages

Corn is used in beer and ale.

Food Acids

Corn is used to make citric acid.

Medicinal Preparations

Corn is used in injections, pills, tablets, and other drugs.

Cooking Oil

  • Corn oil: A common cooking oil made from corn germ, a side product of corn milling.
  • Phytosterols: A natural plant substance in corn oil that helps reduce cholesterol absorption.
  • Ubiquinone (CoQ10): A vitamin in corn oil that may help reduce the risk of heart damage.

Thickener

Cornstarch: A thickener used in sauces, dessert mixes, and custard powders.

Corn is also high in fiber and contains carotenoids similar to vitamin A. These nutrients can help with digestive health, eye health, and weight loss.

Common Diseases

Corn crops can be affected by many diseases, including leaf blights, stalk rots, and ear rots.

Leaf Blights

  • Anthracnose leaf blight: Small, oval to elongated lesions with tan to brown centers and yellow to reddish brown borders.
  • Gray leaf spot: A foliar disease that can build up in systems of corn monoculture.
  • Northern corn leaf blight: Long, elliptical to cigar-shaped, gray-green lesions that become tan, brown.
  • Common rust: Small, cinnamon-brown, powdery pustules that rupture the leaf surface.
  • Eyespot: Small, circular to oval, translucent lesions surrounded by yellow to purple margins.

Pesticides and Fertilizers

Pesticides used for corn crops in India include herbicides and insecticides.

Herbicides

  • Glyphosate: An active ingredient in herbicides like Roundup, this herbicide is used to reduce surrounding growth.
