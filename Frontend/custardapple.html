Custard Apple Plant Uses

Custard apple is a subtropical fruit crop known for its fresh consumption, flavoring, and medicinal properties. It is also called Sitaphal or Shareefa in India.

Uses

  • Fresh Consumption: Custard apples are eaten fresh and are a popular fruit in domestic and international markets.
  • Flavoring: Custard apple pulp is used to flavor ice cream, kheer, and milkshakes.
  • Medicinal Purposes: Every part of the custard apple tree has medicinal properties.
  • Vermicidal & Insecticidal Properties: The green fruit and seeds have effective vermicidal and insecticidal properties.
  • Astringent: The bark, leaves, unripe fruit, and seeds contain the alkaloid anonaine.
  • Purgative: The roots of custard apple trees have strong purgative properties.
  • Soap & Cattle Feed: The seeds are used in soap production, while seed cake is used as cattle feed.

Farming

Custard apple farming is becoming a lucrative option for farmers, especially in semi-arid and tropical regions. With sustainable farming practices, cultivating custard apples can improve farmers' incomes and contribute to ecological balance. It is a drought-hardy tree and well-suited for dryland cultivation.

Common Diseases

  • Anthracnose: A common fungal disease of custard apples.
  • Black Canker: Causes damage to the tree bark and branches.
  • Diplodia Rot: A fungal disease that affects the fruit.
  • Leaf Spot: Causes dark spots on leaves and fruit, affecting plant health.
  • Mealy Bug: A common pest that affects custard apple plants.
  • Spiral Nematode: A parasitic nematode that damages roots.
  • Other Nematodes: Various nematodes can impact plant growth and fruit quality.

Disease Management

  • Expert Systems: Can diagnose custard apple diseases and recommend treatments.
  • Machine Learning: Helps identify and classify diseases using datasets.
  • Neem Seed Kernel Extract & Bacillus Subtilis: Environmentally sustainable alternatives to chemical pesticides.
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM): Helps reduce the negative impact of chemical pesticides.

Other Issues

  • Nitrogen Deficiency: Affects the overall growth and fruit quality of custard apples.
  • Weevil (Orthorhinus cylindrirostris): A pest that can damage custard apple plants.

Pesticides & Fertilizers

Neem Oil

  • Spot spraying neem oil and neem seed kernel extract can suppress pests and encourage natural control.

Synthetic Insecticides

  • Quinalphos, Triazophos, and Dimethoate are synthetic insecticides used to treat mealy bugs and other pests.

Biopesticides from Custard Apple Seeds

  • The oil from custard apple seeds can be extracted using solvents like hexane or methanol.
  • The extracted oil is used as a natural insecticide.
  • The seeds are effective against head lice and fleas.
  • Anosom: A biological pesticide that contains squamosin (annonin) as an active ingredient.

Custard Apple Extracts

  • Leaf Extract: Targets aphids, brown plant hoppers, caterpillars, and other pests.
  • Seed Extract: Used against ants and aphids.
  • Seed Oil Extract: Effective against diamondback moth pupae.
