Guava Plant Uses

Guava cultivation is important for the production of nutritious fruit that can be eaten fresh or processed into many foods. Guava leaves are also used for medicinal purposes.

Uses of Guava Fruit

  • Food: Guava fruit is eaten fresh or used to make jams, jellies, preserves, nectars, juices, and smoothies.
  • Pastry Fillings: Guava is a common filling for pastries.
  • Ice Cream: Guava pulp is used to make ice cream, milkshakes, and guava-flavored yogurts.
  • Beverages: Guava pulp is used to make cocktails, fruit drinks, and smoothies.

Uses of Guava Leaves

  • Medicine: Guava leaves are used to treat diarrhea, pain, diabetes, and wound healing.
  • Dyeing and Tanning: Guava leaves are used for dyeing and tanning.

Guava Cultivation

Guava is grown in tropical and subtropical areas around the world. It can be grown in a variety of soil types, including poor to alkaline soils. Guava trees can be pruned to regulate crop yield. The plant is a primary host of the Caribbean fruit fly and can also be grown in pots indoors.

Common Diseases

Guava plants can be affected by many diseases, including wilt, anthracnose, canker, leaf spots, and fruit rots.

Diseases of Guava

  • Wilt: Caused by the fungus Fusarium oxysporum f. sp. psidii
  • Anthracnose: Caused by the fungus Gloeosporium psidii
  • Canker: Caused by the fungi Physalospora psidii and Pestalotiopsis psidii
  • Algal Leaf and Fruit Spot: Caused by the alga Cephaleuros virescens
  • Red Rust: Caused by the alga Cephaleuros virescens
  • Styler End Rot: Caused by the fungus Phomopsis psidii
  • Damping Off: Caused by the fungus Rhizoctonia solani
  • Rust: Caused by the fungus Puccinia psidii
  • Fruit Rot: Caused by various microorganisms, including Cylindrocladium, Lasiodiplodia, Mucor, and Rhizopus species.

Disease Management

  • Prune affected branches to improve air circulation and sunlight penetration.
  • Apply sulfur-based fungicides as a preventive measure.
  • Use neem oil to manage powdery mildew.

Pesticides/Fertilizers

Pesticides used for guava crop cultivation include insecticides, fungicides, and bait sprays.

Insecticides

  • Malathion: Used to control fruit borers
  • Fipronil: Used to control fruit borers
  • Emamectin Benzoate: Used to control fruit borers
  • Phosphamidon: Used to control insect pests
  • Monocrotophos: Used to control insect pests
  • Dimethoate: Used to control insect pests
  • Pyrellin: Registered for use on guavas
  • Provado: Registered for use on guavas
  • Spin Tor 2SC: Registered for use on guavas

Fungicides

  • Carbendazim: Used to control anthracnose disease
  • Thiophanate Methyl: Used to control anthracnose disease
  • Kavach: Used to control anthracnose disease
  • Mancozeb: Used to control anthracnose disease
  • Copper Oxychloride: Used to control anthracnose disease
  • Azoxystrobin: Used to control anthracnose disease
  • Difenoconazole: Used to control anthracnose disease

Bait Sprays

  • Methyl Eugenol: Used to monitor and kill fruit flies
  • Molasses or Jaggery: Used in bait sprays with insecticides

Other Management Practices

  • Collecting and destroying fallen and infested fruits.
  • Summer ploughing to expose pupa.
  • Field release of parasitoids.
