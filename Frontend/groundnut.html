Groundnut Plant Uses

Groundnuts have many uses, including food, animal feed, and soil improvement.

Food

  • Peanut butter: Made by grinding roasted and blanched groundnut kernels.
  • Roasted peanuts: A snack food made from raw groundnut kernels.
  • Confections: Made from groundnuts.
  • Nutritional bars: Made from groundnuts.
  • Chocolate: Made from groundnuts.
  • Laddoos: Made from groundnuts.
  • Soups and desserts: Made with groundnuts.

Animal Feed

  • Groundnut meal: A by-product of oil extraction, used as animal feed.
  • Oil cake: A by-product of oil extraction, used as animal feed.

Soil Improvement

  • Organic manure: Groundnuts can be used as organic manure to improve soil fertility.
  • Nitrogen fixation: Groundnuts improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Other Uses

  • Cooking oil: Groundnut oil is used for cooking, frying, and salad dressings.
  • Margarine: Made from groundnut oil.
  • Cosmetic products: Made from groundnut oil.
  • Activated carbon: Made from groundnut shells.
  • Bio-fuel: Groundnut shells are used as a renewable energy source.

Groundnuts are also rich in vitamins and minerals, and contain antioxidants that may help reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

Common Diseases

Groundnut crops can be affected by a variety of diseases, including viral, fungal, bacterial, and nematode diseases.

Viral Diseases

  • Bud necrosis disease: Caused by the groundnut bud necrosis virus (GBNV), which is transmitted by thrips. This disease causes the terminal bud to die, which can lead to significant economic losses.
  • Tomato spotted wilt virus: Causes bud necrosis disease in groundnut, which can lead to severe yield losses.

Fungal Diseases

  • Stem rot: Caused by the fungus Sclerotium rolfsii, which can cause the stem of the groundnut plant to rot and die.
  • Early leaf spot: Caused by the fungus Cercospora arachidicola.
  • Late leaf spot: Caused by the fungus Phaeoisariopsis personata, also known as "tikka disease."
  • Sclerotinia blight: Caused by the soil-borne fungi Sclerotinia minor and Sclerotinia sclerotiorum.

Bacterial Diseases

  • Bacterial wilt: Caused by Pseudomonas solanacearum.

Nematode Diseases

Root-knot nematodes, root lesion nematodes, ring nematodes, sting nematodes, and testa nematodes.

Other Diseases

Rust, collar rot, aflaroot, pod rot, and Alternaria leaf spot.

Pesticides and Fertilizers

Pesticides used for groundnut crops include herbicides, fungicides, and insecticides.

Herbicides

  • Odyssey: An herbicide that controls broadleaf weeds and grasses in groundnuts.
  • Imazethapyr: A herbicide that controls major weeds in groundnuts.
  • Pendimethalin: A herbicide that controls major weeds in groundnuts.
  • Diclosulam: A herbicide that controls major weeds in groundnuts.
  • Quizalofop-ethyl: A herbicide that controls major weeds in groundnuts.

Fungicides

  • Xelora: A systemic fungicide used for seed treatment.
  • Priaxor: A fungicide that controls diseases in groundnuts.

Insecticides

  • Dichlorovos: An insecticide used to control storage pests.
  • Malathion: An insecticide used to control storage pests.
  • Fenitrothion: An insecticide used to control storage pests.
  • Deltamethrin: An insecticide used to control storage pests.
  • Chlorpyrifos: An insecticide used to control termites and other soil pests.
  • Isofenphos: An insecticide used to control termites and other soil pests.
  • Carbosulfan: An insecticide used to control leaf miners.
  • Phorate: An insecticide used to control leaf miners.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) can also be used to manage pests in groundnut crops.
