Pineapple Plant Uses

Economic Benefits

  • Income: Pineapple cultivation can be a profitable business.
  • Demand: There is a large demand for pineapple in India, especially for canned slices and juice.

Uses of the Fruit

  • Fresh Consumption: Pineapples are eaten fresh.
  • Processed Products: Used to make juice, jam, squash, syrup, pulp, nectar, and leather.
  • Medicinal Uses: Pineapples aid digestion and act as a natural anti-inflammatory due to bromelain.
  • Decorations: Pineapple plants with the crown intact are used for decorative purposes.
  • Other Uses: Pineapple juice is used as a diuretic, for sore throat relief, as an antidote for sea sickness, and more.

Common Diseases

  • Leaf Rot: Caused by Phytophthora nicotianae, leading to dead areas on leaves.
  • Heart Rot: Causes water-soaked lesions and a rotten smell in the central stem.
  • Black Rot: Also called water blister, caused by Chalara paradoxa, leading to fruit destruction.
  • Other Diseases: Fruitlet core rot, Fusariosis, Green fruit rot, Leathery pocket, White leaf spot, and more.

Pests

Common pests affecting pineapples include:

  • Mealy bugs
  • Scale insects
  • Thrips
  • Fruit borer
  • Bud moths
  • Fruit flies
  • White grubs
  • Beetles
  • Weevils

Pesticides and Fertilizers

Herbicides

  • Bromocil: Used as a pre-emergent treatment or spray.
  • Diuron: Used as a pre-emergent spray.
  • Glyphosate: Used along with hand weeding.

Insecticides

  • Monocrotophos: Protects against mealy bugs.
  • Quinalphos: Protects against mealy bugs.

Fungicides

  • Carbendazim: Protects against heart rot.

Other Chemicals

  • Hilban: Used for dipping suckers.
  • Indofil: Used for dipping suckers.
  • Diazinon: An organophosphorus insecticide.
  • Ethoprophos: An organophosphorus insecticide.

Other Pesticide-Related Issues

  • Pesticide residues have been detected in water from pineapple farms.
  • Agrochemical use has been linked to health problems.
  • Some pineapple companies have been accused of contaminating water sources.
